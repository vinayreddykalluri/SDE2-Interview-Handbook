# Advanced Review: SDE-2 Leadership

## Advanced model

| Signal | Strong evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Ambiguity | Converted unclear goals into decisions, milestones, risks |
| Judgment | Compared options with data and documented trade-offs |
| Influence | Changed direction without relying on authority |
| Execution | Managed dependencies, quality, scope, and delivery |
| Incidents | Stabilized, communicated, learned, prevented recurrence |
| Leverage | Mentored, automated, documented, or simplified |
| Product thinking | Connected engineering to user/business outcomes |
| Growth | Changed behavior after failure or feedback |

## Reconstruction exercise

Create a six-story map. For each, write stakes, your decision, rejected alternative, conflict, measurable outcome, mistake, and later learning. This preserves evidence without memorized scripts.

## Drill ladder

- **Foundation:** deliver a two-minute STAR story with clear ownership.
- **SDE-2:** handle five minutes of technical follow-ups.
- **Advanced:** explain a failure your decision contributed to.
- **Transfer:** answer three prompts using the same evidence naturally.

## Retrieval prompts

1. What decision did you personally make?
2. Which stakeholder concern was valid?
3. What metric proves the outcome?
4. Which quality guardrail did you preserve?
5. How did the work improve team leverage?
6. What learning changed a later decision?
7. How would a teammate describe your weakest action?
8. Where did you trade delivery against long-term cost?

## Exit criteria

Deliver six consistent stories with measurable impact, technical depth, accountable failure, and unscripted follow-up handling.
