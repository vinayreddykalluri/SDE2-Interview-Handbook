# Leadership Evidence and Story Bank

## Required stories

| Signal | Prompt seed | Evidence to include |
| --- | --- | --- |
| End-to-end ownership | Ambiguous project you drove | Scope, plan, risks, outcome |
| Technical judgment | Architecture trade-off | Alternatives, constraints, decision |
| Incident leadership | Production failure | Triage, communication, prevention |
| Conflict and influence | Disagreement without authority | Listening, data, alignment |
| Delivery under constraints | Deadline or resource pressure | Prioritization, guardrails, result |
| Mentoring and leverage | Raised team capability | Feedback, docs, automation, impact |

## Story quality checklist

- Use `I` for your decisions and `we` for shared outcomes.
- State scale, stakes, users, and constraints in the first minute.
- Explain at least one rejected alternative.
- Include metrics before and after when available.
- Name a mistake or uncertainty without disguising it as a strength.
- Close with a learning and later changed behavior.

## Follow-up preparation

Expect questions about the hardest technical detail, stakeholder disagreement, what you would do differently, measurement, post-launch failure, and how another engineer would describe your role.
